Whether you’re a seasoned camper or a first-timer, understanding the costs associated with renting a small RV is crucial for planning a successful trip. How Much Does a Small RV Cost to Rent?#

Thinking about embarking on a road trip adventure in a small RV? Before hitting the open road, you’ll want to have a clear picture of the rental costs involved. Let’s dive into the factors that influence the rental price and provide some tips for finding the best deals.

Factors Affecting Rental Cost:

  • Size Matters: Generally, the larger the RV, the higher the rental cost. Small RVs, typically ranging from 17 to 24 feet in length, offer a cozy and efficient option for couples or small families. For larger groups or extended trips, consider a mid-size or large RV, but be prepared for a higher rental fee.

  • Amenities and Features: Just like hotel rooms, RVs come with varying levels of amenities and features. Air conditioning, a refrigerator, a microwave, and a bathroom are standard features, but you might have to pay extra for extras like a generator, a satellite TV, or a DVD player.

  • Rental Duration: The cost of renting a small RV typically follows a simple principle: the longer the rental period, the lower the daily rate. Longer rentals often come with discounted weekly or monthly rates, making them more cost-effective for extended trips.

  • Location and Seasonality: Rental prices can vary depending on the location and time of year. During peak travel seasons (summer and holidays), demand for RVs is higher, leading to increased rental rates. Renting during the off-season (fall and spring) can often result in lower costs.

  • Insurance and Security Deposits: Most rental companies require you to purchase insurance to protect the vehicle during the rental period. Additionally, a security deposit is generally required to cover any potential damages or cleaning fees.

Tips for Finding the Best Deals:

  • Shop Around and Compare Prices: Don’t settle for the first rental company you find. Take your time to compare prices and offerings from multiple companies. Online platforms like RVshare and Outdoorsy allow you to compare RVs and rental rates from different providers.

  • Consider Private RV Rentals: In addition to traditional rental companies, consider exploring private RV rental options through platforms like RVshare or Craigslist. Private owners often offer competitive rates and a wider selection of RVs. However, be sure to carefully review the terms and conditions and inspect the RV thoroughly before finalizing the rental.

  • Look for Discounts and Special Offers: Keep an eye out for discounts and special offers from rental companies, especially during the off-season or for extended rentals. You might find promotional rates, loyalty programs, or discounts for AAA members.

  • Negotiate the Price: Don’t be afraid to negotiate the rental price, especially if you’re renting for a longer period or during the off-season. Politely inquire about any potential discounts or additional services that might be included in the quoted price.

Tips for finding the best deal on a small RV rental#

Renting a small RV is a great way to explore the country and have your own home on wheels. But before you hit the open road, you need to know how much it’s going to cost.

In this article, I’ll share a few tips on how to find the best deal on a small RV rental.

1. Consider the size of the RV you need#

The first thing you need to think about is the size of the RV you need. If you’re traveling alone or with a small group, a compact or mid-size RV will probably be enough. These RVs are usually easy to drive and can find parking easily. However, if you’re traveling with a large group or you need more space, you’ll need to rent a larger RV.

2. Choose your amenities wisely#

The amenities that are included in your RV rental can also affect the price. If you’re looking for a basic RV with just a few amenities, you’ll pay less than if you rent an RV with all the bells and whistles.

When choosing amenities, think about what’s important to you and what you can live without. For example, if you’re planning on doing a lot of cooking, you’ll want an RV with a well-equipped kitchen. But if you’re planning on eating out most of the time, you can save money by renting an RV with a smaller kitchen.

3. Decide how long you want to rent the RV#

The length of your rental period can also affect the price. The longer you rent the RV, the more you’ll pay.

If you’re only planning on taking a short trip, you can probably get away with renting an RV for a few days or a week. But if you’re planning on taking a longer trip, you’ll need to rent the RV for a month or more.

**4. Tips for finding the best deal on a small RV rental#

  • Shop around. Compare prices from different rental companies before you make a decision. You can use online resources, like RVshare or Outdoorsy, to compare rates and find the best deal.

  • Be flexible with your travel dates. If you can, try to avoid renting an RV during peak season. This will help you save money.

  • Consider renting from a private owner. Renting from a private owner can often be cheaper than renting from a rental company. However, you’ll need to do your research to make sure you’re renting from a reputable owner.

  • Look for discounts. Many rental companies offer discounts for AAA members, military members, and students. Be sure to ask about discounts when you’re booking your rental.

Key Takeaways:#

  • Consider the size of the RV and choose amenities wisely to optimize cost-effectiveness.

  • Longer rental periods typically incur higher costs.

  • Flexibility in travel dates and renting from private owners can lead to savings.

  • Comparing prices, seeking discounts, and ensuring reputable sources are key to finding the best deal.

Checklist for renting a small RV#

Choosing the best RV for your travel adventure can be overwhelming. Here’s a simple guide and checklist to simplify your RV rental experience.

Essential Considerations

  • Type of RV: Choose between Class A (motorhome), Class B (camper van), and Class C (crossover). Each type offers varying levels of comfort and suitability for different travel styles.
  • Size: Consider the number of people you’ll travel with and the space you’ll need for sleeping, cooking, and relaxing. Measure the total length, width, and height to ensure it fits your travel route.
  • Amenities: Decide on the amenities essential to your trip, such as a kitchen, bathroom, air conditioning, and heating. Check the RV’s specs to confirm availability.
  • Duration: Determine the rental period based on your trip length. Whether it’s a weekend getaway or a cross-country journey, plan a suitable duration to make the most of your adventure.

Checklist for Rental

  1. Choose a Reputable Company: Research and compare different RV rental companies. Read reviews, verify their licensing and insurance, and ensure they offer roadside assistance and 24/7 support.

  2. Inspect the RV Thoroughly: Before signing the contract, inspect the RV for any damages, mechanical issues, and ensure all appliances and amenities are in working condition. Document any concerns with the rental company.

  3. Understand Rental Contract: Read and understand the rental contract, including terms, conditions, insurance coverage, and any additional fees or charges. Know your responsibilities and limitations as a renter.

  4. Confirm Insurance Coverage: Ensure you have adequate insurance coverage. Discuss with the rental company if additional insurance is available or if you need to provide proof of your own.

  5. Pack Smart: Pack only essential items to save space and reduce weight. Consider bringing bedding, towels, toiletries, cooking supplies, and outdoor gear.

  6. Plan Your Route: Research your travel route, including campgrounds, rest stops, and attractions. Have a map or GPS device handy to navigate confidently.

  7. Prepare for Emergencies: Pack a first aid kit, emergency supplies, flashlights, and a fire extinguisher. Keep important documents like IDs, insurance, and rental contract safely stored.

Key Takeaways:

  • Choosing an RV depends on the type, size, amenities, and duration of your trip.
  • Thoroughly research and compare RV rental companies for reliability and customer support.
  • Carefully inspect the RV before renting to identify any issues or damages.
  • Read and understand the rental contract to know your responsibilities and limitations.
  • Confirm insurance coverage to protect yourself and the RV during your rental period.
  • Pack smart, plan your route, and prepare for emergencies to ensure a smooth and enjoyable RV adventure.

Additional considerations for renting a small RV#

Deciding on an RV rental duration and size hinges on several factors. Here’s what to consider:

Duration of the Rental#

There’s no one-size-fits-all approach to RV rentals. The ideal length depends on your itinerary, budget, and flexibility:

  1. Short-Term Rentals (1-3 Days):

    • Perfect for weekend getaways or short road trips.
    • May incur higher daily rates but lower weekly or monthly totals.

  2. Mid-Term Rentals (1-2 Weeks):

    • Suitable for longer road trips or camping adventures.
    • Balance cost and flexibility.

  3. Long-Term Rentals (1 Month or More):

    • Ideal for extended stays or cross-country trips.
    • Often offer discounted monthly rates.

Size of the RV#

RV sizes vary widely, from compact Class B vans to spacious Class A motorhomes. Matching size to needs is crucial:

  1. Compact RVs (Under 20 Feet):

    • Ideal for solo travelers or couples seeking maneuverability.
    • May have limited space and amenities.

  2. Mid-Size RVs (20-25 Feet):

    • Suitable for small families or groups of friends.
    • Offer more space and amenities than compact RVs.

  3. Large RVs (Over 25 Feet):

    • Ideal for large families or groups seeking comfort and luxury.
    • Offer ample space and amenities, including sleeping areas, kitchens, and bathrooms.

Amenities and Features#

Consider the amenities and features that matter to you:

  1. Sleeping Arrangements:

    • Assess the number and size of beds.
    • Consider sleeping arrangements for children.

  2. Kitchen Facilities:

    • Check for a stove, refrigerator, and sink.
    • Consider features like a microwave or oven.

  3. Bathroom Amenities:

    • Ensure there’s a toilet, sink, and shower.
    • Check for features like a separate toilet and shower.

  4. Entertainment and Technology:

    • Look for a TV, DVD player, or sound system.
    • Consider features like GPS navigation or satellite radio.

  5. Outdoor Features:

    • Check for an awning, patio furniture, or a grill.
    • Consider features like rooftop solar panels or a generator.

Additional Costs#

  1. Fees and Taxes:

    • Be aware of rental fees, taxes, and surcharges.
    • Some states have additional fees for RV rentals.

  2. Insurance:

    • Consider purchasing insurance to protect yourself and the RV.
    • Check your personal auto insurance policy for coverage.

  3. Mileage Fees:

    • Some rental companies charge extra for exceeding a certain mileage limit.
    • Plan your trip carefully to avoid excess mileage charges.

  4. Cleaning Fees:

    • Many rental companies charge a cleaning fee at the end of your rental.
    • Clean the RV thoroughly before returning it to avoid additional charges.

  5. Security Deposits:

    • Most rental companies require a security deposit before you can rent an RV.
    • This deposit is typically refundable after your rental period.

Key Takeaways:#

  • Consider the duration of your rental to find the best rates and flexibility.

  • Choose an RV size that suits your group size, needs, and comfort level.

  • Prioritize amenities and features that matter most to you.

  • Be aware of additional costs like fees, taxes, insurance, mileage, cleaning, and security deposits.

  • Research and compare prices from different rental companies to find the best deal.
