Business Structure and Overhead Costs#

In the Lone Star State, the business structure of your General Contracting firm can have a major impact on your earnings. The two primary structures to choose from are sole proprietorship and limited liability company (LLC). As a sole proprietor, you’ll have complete control over your business and its profits, but you’ll also be personally liable for any debts or liabilities. An LLC, on the other hand, offers more protection for your personal assets, but it’s also subject to more complex tax rules.

The structure you choose will affect your overhead costs as well. If you operate as a sole proprietor, your overhead will likely be lower since you won’t have to pay certain fees and taxes that LLCs are required to pay. However, if you’re working on larger projects that require more employees and equipment, your overhead costs will likely be higher than an LLC.

Key Takeaways:

  • The business structure you choose will influence your earnings as a General Contractor in Texas.
  • Sole proprietorship offers complete control but entails personal liability.
  • Limited Liability Company (LLC) provides asset protection but has complex tax rules.
  • Business structure impacts overhead costs, including fees and taxes.
  • Larger projects and increased manpower raise overhead expenses.
